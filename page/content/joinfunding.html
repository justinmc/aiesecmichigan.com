Looking for Funding?

Many sources of funding are available for Michigan students going abroad. The following list shows a few of the funds that many students going abroad through AIESEC qualify for.


Raoul Wallenberg International Summer Travel Fellowship
  • Who: All undergraduate and graduate students
  • Where: Anywhere in the world
  • For What: Community service or civic participation
  • Covers: Transportation and room and board
  • How Much: Up to $5000
  • Deadline: February 22
  • More info: Contact Ginsberg Center, 647-7402; e-mail Ardele Stewart at ardy@umich.edu

International Institute Individual Fellowship (IIIF)
  • Who: University of Michigan undergraduate, graduate, and professional school students are eligible, regardless of citizenship. Applicants must be enrolled in a degree program. Ph.D. candidates and students graduating before the start of the project are not eligible. Eligible internship or research projects must be at least one month in length, constitute at least a 30-hour-per-week time commitment (exclusive of language training), and meet all requirements of the University Travel Policy and the Institutional Review Board.
  • Where: Anywhere in the world
  • For What: Internships or research
  • Covers: General costs
  • How Much: Up to $5000
  • Deadline: Mid February
  • More info: Visit their website or contact Kelly Peckens (kpeckens@umich.edu)

Center for International and Comparative Studies (CICS)
  • Who: University of Michigan undergraduate students. Certain funding is restricted to International Studies concentrators and minors.
  • Where: Anywhere in the world.
  • For What: Internship support and research fellowships, preferably with a focus on human rights, international development, or international security and cooperation.
  • Covers: Travel and living expenses.
  • How Much: Up to $2000 for undergraduates, up to $4500 for graduates.
  • What's the Catch? Grantees will be required to provide a brief report outlining the experience and/or discussing findings. This report may be used in CICS publications. Grantees are also encouraged to submit photographs to be considered for publication in CICS materials. See individual award announcements for additional requirements.
  • Deadline: Typically mid-March. Check the CICS funding page for details.
  • More info: The CICS funding page has all contact information and other details.
