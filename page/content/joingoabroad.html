So, what's it like going abroad through AIESEC?

Because we are an organization run entirely by student volunteers, AIESEC interns experience a level of immersion not found in most exchange programs. From the minute you step off the plane, you will be completely taken care of by the students in your destination's chapter of AIESEC.

This host chapter is just like us here at AIESEC Michigan; a group of students dedicated to promoting internatioanl exchange into and out of our home countries. They will help you find housing for your stay, ensure that you can commute between home and work, and help you with everything you need while on your internship. Best of all, they are young college aged students just like you, excited about meeting new people, showing them around town, and having a good time.

Anything that needs to be taken care of before your host chapter can help will be done by us at AIESEC Michigan, your home chapter. You will be assigned an individual experienced member to help you through the process of finding your ideal internship, applying, and handling all visa and other requirements before you leave. We also hold several sessions to help teach everyone how to use the database effectively and make sure everything is going smoothly. Soon enough after, you'll be hearing back from companies about your applications and talking to your host chapter about what it will be like to live in their country.

What are the internships like?

All AIESEC internships are set up individually on a case-by-case basis for the needs of the company, so each one is unique. Length can vary anywhere from 6 weeks to 2 years, and location can be anywhere in our network of 107 different countries on 6 continents. Some are paid, some are volunteer, and you could be working anywhere from the smallest nonprofit organization to the largest multinational corporation.

Thankfully, all of this variety is managed by our online internship database. After being accepted by AIESEC Michigan, we will set you up on this database and teach you how to use it at every step. You'll be able to search for the internships that fit you best, and then apply to each one individually. Final acceptance at this stage is handled entirely by the company, so requirements obviously vary depending on the job. Application is done by sending a resume and cover letter, and many companies also interview candidates. From here your chances of being accepted are comparable to applying to a similar job in the US based on your skills; however, many companies do favor American applicants due to speaking native English.

The types of internships that are available on the database are also incredibly varied. Because the AIESEC network is so huge, chances are if you're looking for some moderately specific field of work, you can find an internship to fit. That said, some internships are more plentiful than others, and availability definitely varies by region. To help in narrowing down your search, the database is divided into four main types of internships.

Developmental Internships

These internships are targeted towards students and graduates in all fields of study, and they generally involve doing volunteer work with AIESEC and related nonprofit organizations. Volunteers are typically provided with accomodation by the local AIESEC chapter, and work often involves community development projects such as raising awareness of social issues such as HIV or green energy.

Education Internships

These are targeted towards students and recent graduates in linguistics, foreign languages, political science, and more. Interns often teach conversational and written English in language schools, social institutions, nonprofits, or companies. They develop team management skills, public speaking experience, and foreign language ability.

Management Internships

Management internships are usually for upperclassmen and graduates in economics, management, international relations, business administration, or marketing. They are available with companies anywhere from start ups to large multinational corporations. Interns often do work in market evaluation and research, international marketing, and public relations.

Technical Internships

These are targeted towards technical students and graduates in fields like engineering, computer science, informatics, and other similar areas. Interns generally work in technology corporations, though IT work is also available in other types of companies.
